FAQ

Home Addition Questions

Straight answers to what homeowners ask most before adding on.

How long does a home addition take?
Most room additions run 3–6 months from foundation to finish. Second-story additions and larger in-law suites can take 4–8 months depending on scope, weather, and permitting timelines. You'll get a written schedule with your estimate.
Will an addition match the rest of my house?
Yes — that's the goal. We match roof lines, siding, trim profiles, windows, and interior finishes so the addition reads as part of the original house, not an obvious bolt-on. For older homes with discontinued materials, we source the closest match available.
Do I need an architect or engineer?
Most additions require stamped structural plans from an engineer, and larger projects benefit from architectural drawings. We coordinate with local architects and engineers we trust, or build from your drawings if you already have them.
Can I stay in my home during the addition?
Yes, in almost every case. We seal off the tie-in wall, control dust, and only open the connection between old and new when we're ready to work there. For second-story additions that require temporary roof work, we plan for weather.
Can you build a second-story addition or garage conversion?
Yes. Second stories, over-garage bonus rooms, garage-to-living conversions, and detached ADUs are all common projects. Each has its own structural, egress, and code considerations — we manage all of it.
Do you pull permits and handle inspections?
Yes. As your general contractor we pull all required Idaho or Washington building permits and coordinate every inspection. Call (208) 494-8818 or request a meeting online to get started.
