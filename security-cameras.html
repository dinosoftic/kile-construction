Three Decades of Construction Meets Modern Home Tech

This is one of the things that makes Kile Construction different. We're not just a remodeling company, and we're not just a big-box install crew. Chuck and Norm Kile have spent more than 30 years in homes across Coeur d'Alene, Hayden, and Post Falls — which means we understand framing, wall cavities, attic paths, and trim details from the inside out.

That background pays off the moment somebody has to run an Ethernet drop through a finished wall, mount a camera on stucco or cedar without tearing it up, or conceal TV wiring in a lived-in home. Most installers avoid that kind of work. We do it every day.

FAQ

Security Cameras & Networking Questions

Straight answers to what homeowners ask most before scheduling a home-tech install.

Do you sell the cameras or do I buy them?
Either works. We can recommend equipment that's reliable and easy to support, or install cameras you've already purchased. Looping us in before you buy usually saves trouble later with power, cable paths, and compatibility.
Can you run Ethernet through a finished house?
Yes. Because we're carpenters and contractors first, we can fish Ethernet through walls, ceilings, and crawlspaces cleanly — and patch any drywall openings as part of the job so there's nothing left for you to finish.
Will the cameras work with my phone?
Yes. We set up your system with the manufacturer's app and walk you through how to view live feeds, pull recorded clips, and manage notifications from your phone or computer.
Can you improve my Wi-Fi without replacing everything?
Often, yes. Better access-point placement, a mesh node, or one well-placed Ethernet drop to a remote access point can dramatically improve coverage without a full network overhaul.
Do you do smart garage door openers and TV mounting?
Yes. We upgrade older garage openers to smart models for app-based control, mount TVs flush with concealed wiring, and install other smart-home devices as part of a home-tech visit.
