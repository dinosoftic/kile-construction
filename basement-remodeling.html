FAQ

Basement Remodeling Questions

Straight answers to what homeowners ask most before finishing a basement.

How long does a basement remodel take?
Most basement finishes take 6–12 weeks depending on square footage and whether you're adding a bathroom, kitchenette, or egress windows. You'll get a written schedule with your estimate.
Do I need egress windows in a finished basement?
Yes — any bedroom below grade requires a code-compliant egress window and window well. We handle the concrete cutting, window install, and permitting so you're fully code-compliant.
Can you add a bathroom or kitchenette downstairs?
Yes. We handle plumbing rough-in, including below-slab work or sewage ejector systems where the waste line is above the basement floor, plus electrical and finishes for basement baths, wet bars, and full kitchenettes.
How do you handle moisture and insulation in a basement?
Proper vapor barrier, rigid foam or closed-cell spray foam on concrete walls, and drainage planning before any framing goes up. Getting this right is the difference between a basement that stays dry for decades and one that molds within a couple of years.
Can I convert the basement into an ADU or in-law suite?
In many cases, yes. We can add a separate entrance, full kitchen, and bathroom — but local zoning, ceiling height, and egress rules dictate what's possible on your specific property. We'll walk through the specifics during the free consultation.
Do you pull permits for a basement remodel?
Yes. Framing, electrical, plumbing, and egress work all require Idaho or Washington permits. We pull them and coordinate every inspection. Call (208) 494-8818 or request a meeting online to get started.
