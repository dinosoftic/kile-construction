Local Experience

Three Decades of Real Woodworking and Finish Work

Kile Construction isn't just installing parts out of a box. Chuck and Norm Kile have spent more than 30 years doing the actual carpentry — hanging doors, replacing windows, running trim, and building finish details in new construction and older Coeur d'Alene homes alike.

That means better fit, better finish, and better decisions when challenges come up inside a real home — walls out of plumb, rough openings out of square, settled floors, or original trim profiles that nobody stocks anymore.

A new door can improve privacy, function, and appearance. Updated trim can make an older room feel finished. Better carpentry can tie a whole remodel together and make everything look intentional instead of patched. These are the upgrades homeowners notice every single day.

FAQ

Doors, Windows & Trim Questions

Straight answers to what homeowners ask most before starting a carpentry project.

Do you install interior and exterior doors?
Yes. We install interior doors, pre-hung doors, entry doors, and specialty doors. Proper shimming, hinge alignment, and trim work are all part of a clean install — not a separate charge.
Can you replace windows in an older home?
Yes. We replace windows in smaller packages — especially common in older Coeur d'Alene homes where rough openings and existing trim need careful attention. Full whole-house window replacements are also available.
What kind of trim work do you do?
Baseboard, casing, crown molding, wainscoting, chair rail, window and door trim, and custom built-up profiles. Everything is cut, coped, glued, and nailed so joints stay tight as the home moves through the seasons.
How long does a door or window install take?
A single interior door swap is typically half a day. An entry door is usually a full day. Window replacements vary by size and quantity but most single-window jobs are completed in a day.
Can you fix a door that won't close or latch?
Yes. Doors that drag, stick, or won't latch usually come down to house settling, hinge alignment, or a rough opening out of square. We diagnose the actual cause and correct it — rather than just sanding the edge of the door.
