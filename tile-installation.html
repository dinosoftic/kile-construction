FAQ

Tile Installation Questions

Straight answers to what homeowners ask most before starting a tile project.

How long does tile installation take?
A typical kitchen backsplash takes 1–3 days. A full shower surround usually runs 3–5 days including waterproofing and grout cure time. Larger floors and whole-bathroom tile packages take longer depending on layout and prep.
Do you waterproof showers before tiling?
Yes, always. We use proper waterproofing membranes behind shower tile so the substrate stays dry and the installation lasts. Cutting corners on waterproofing is the main reason bad shower tile fails within a few years.
Can you help pick out tile?
Yes. We can point you to local showrooms, help you avoid tile that's difficult to work with, and give feedback on layout, grout color, and trim pieces so the finished look matches what you had in mind.
Can you tile over existing tile or do you need to demo first?
In most cases, a proper installation means removing the old tile and preparing a fresh substrate. Tiling over old tile can work in specific situations but is usually not the best long-term choice — we'll walk through the options honestly.
Do you do small tile repairs?
Sometimes — it depends on the scope and whether replacement tile is still available. We'll give you an honest read on whether a repair will look right or whether it makes more sense to redo the area.
